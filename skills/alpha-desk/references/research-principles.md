## Active Principles

### 1. Secular trends over noise
Follow multi-year tailwinds (AI infrastructure, energy transition, India digitization). Daily headlines are noise unless they change the structural story.

**Track record**: New — unvalidated.

### 2. Asymmetric risk-reward (3:1 minimum)
Only enter trades where the potential upside is 3x the downside. If the math doesn't work, skip — no matter how good the story sounds.

**Track record**: New — unvalidated.

### 3. Sector leaders capture disproportionate gains
When a theme works, the dominant player (#1 or #2 by market share) gets the lion's share. Don't bet on the 5th player hoping for a catch-up trade.

**Track record**: New — unvalidated.

### 4. Contrarian on panic, not on trend
Buy quality companies on overreaction drops (earnings miss by 5%, stock drops 15%). Don't buy things in secular decline hoping for a reversal.

**Track record**: New — unvalidated.

### 5. Earnings confirm or deny the thesis
The thesis is about the business and its trajectory. Earnings are data points that validate or invalidate. One miss doesn't kill a thesis; two misses demand reassessment.

**Track record**: New — unvalidated.

### 6. Volume confirms direction
Breakouts on low volume are unreliable. Drops on low volume are less concerning. High-volume moves deserve attention.

**Track record**: New — unvalidated.

### 7. Respect the macro regime
When the tide is rising (loose policy, risk-on), be aggressive. When it's falling (tightening, risk-off), be defensive. Stock picking matters less than regime awareness.

**Track record**: New — unvalidated.

### 8. India is not the US
Different monetary cycle (RBI ≠ Fed), different seasonal patterns (monsoon, festivals), different flow dynamics (FII vs DII). Never blindly apply US logic to Indian stocks.

**Track record**: New — unvalidated.

### 9. Position sizing IS the risk management
A great thesis with 50% of capital is reckless. A mediocre thesis with 3% of capital is fine. Size positions by conviction AND by what you can afford to lose.

**Track record**: New — unvalidated.

### 10. Cash is a position
Not being invested IS a decision. When no setup is clear, sitting in cash is the highest-conviction trade. Don't force entries to "stay active."

**Track record**: New — unvalidated.

## Evolution Rules

- **Validate**: After 5+ trades using a principle, mark track record as "validated" or "questionable"
- **Question**: 3+ losses from a principle = move to "under review", test the opposite
- **Codify**: 3+ wins from a new pattern = create a new principle
- **Retire**: Consistently wrong principles move to "Retired" section with explanation
- **Refine**: Vague principles get sub-rules added from specific examples
